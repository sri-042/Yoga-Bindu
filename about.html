Yoga : The word ‘Yoga’ is derived from the Sanskrit root ‘Yuj’, meaning ‘to join’ or ‘to yoke’ or ‘to unite’. yoga is an ancient practice that brings together mind and body. It incorporates breathing exercises, meditation and poses designed to encourage relaxation and reduce stress.

There are mainly 4 ways of attaining yoga

Karma Yoga


Karma yoga : The path of unselfish action is known as Karma Yoga. It teaches that a spiritual seeker sholid act according to dharma, without being attached to the fruits or personal consequences.
As said in Bhagavath geetha :-

"कर्मण्येवाधिकारस्ते मा फलेषु कदाचन ।
मा कर्मफलहेतुर्भुर्मा ते संगोऽस्त्वकर्मणि ॥"

Meaning : You have a right to perform your prescribed duty, but you are not entitled to the fruits of action. Never consider yourself to be the cause of the reslits of your activities, and never be attached to not doing your duty.



Bhakti Yoga

Bhakti Yoga : The path of devotion, the method of attaining God through love and the loving recollection of God is Bhakti Yoga. The Sanskrit word bhakti is derived from the root bhaj, which means "attachment, devotion to, faith or love to something spiritual to attain moksha.

Raja Yoga

Raja Yoga : Raja means King or chief. Raja yoga is the king of all yogas. Raja Yoga is also known as Ashtanga Yoga because its practices can be divided into eight limbs, each limb developed to bring the body and thought energy under control.
The 8 limbs are :

  1. Yama
  2. Niyama
  3. Aasana
  4. Pranayama
  5. Pratyahara
  6. Dharana
  7. Dhyana
  8. Samadhi


Jnana Yoga

Jnana Yoga : “Awareness of absolute consciousness” , this is the definition of Jnana Yoga. It is known as the Yoga of Wisdom.
It concentrates on one-pointed meditation on a single question of self-inquiry to remove the illusions created by an individual.
In the Bhagavad Gita, the main goal of Jnana yoga is self realization.
